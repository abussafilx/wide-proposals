International Contractor Engagement — NES Canada
Section 01
Context & Recommended Structure
How Wide Brazil supports this engagement

NES Canada requires a compliant structure to engage an independent contractor internationally, with the operational, fiscal, and mobility support needed to keep the arrangement running smoothly throughout the assignment.

Wide Brazil will act as the administrative and support partner for this engagement, processing the contractor's payments according to the agreed rate structure, and providing the mobility, fiscal, and welfare support the assignment requires. This structure is designed for engagements where the professional is compensated on a variable, activity-based basis — hourly work, night differentials, per diem, and travel — rather than a fixed monthly salary, and where the company does not intend to establish a local employment relationship.

Recommended Structure

Independent Contractor engagement, administered by Wide Brazil. Wide Brazil processes contractor payments per the agreed rate card, and separately provides visa/immigration support, fiscal guidance, international health coverage, life insurance, and local support for the duration of the assignment.

Payment Administration

Contractor payments are processed against reported hours, per diem days, and travel days, with full traceability for both NES Canada and the contractor.

Immigration & Fiscal Support

Visa/residence matters and fiscal guidance for the expatriate are coordinated directly by Wide Brazil's local team, reducing exposure for NES Canada.

Welfare Coverage

International health plan and life insurance are arranged and maintained for the duration of the engagement.

Local Support

The contractor has a point of contact in Brazil for day-to-day administrative and documentation matters throughout the assignment.

Scope of Services
What Wide Brazil will do
Visa & Immigration Support
✓
Visa / residence process management — advising on the applicable category, formalizing documentation, and filing with the relevant immigration authorities.
✓
Case follow-up — tracking the application through to approval and coordinating retrieval of the visa where applicable.
✓
Personal documentation support — assistance with the registrations required for the contractor to operate locally during the assignment.
Fiscal Support for the Expatriate
✓
Tax guidance — orientation on the fiscal obligations arising from the contractor's international assignment.
✓
Coordination with local tax matters — support in aligning payment administration with applicable fiscal requirements.
✓
Tax orientation — guidance for the contractor on Brazilian tax obligations arising from the assignment.
✓
Payment guide (DARF) preparation — completion of the applicable payment guides for monthly tax settlement.
✓
Annual income tax return — preparation and filing of the contractor's annual Brazilian income tax return (Declaração de Imposto de Renda).
Welfare & Local Support
✓
International health plan — coverage arranged for the contractor for the duration of the assignment.
✓
Life insurance — coverage in place for the length of the engagement.
✓
Local support — a Wide Brazil point of contact available for administrative, documentation, and day-to-day matters throughout the assignment.
Payment Administration
✓
Monthly payment processing — calculated against reported hours, night differentials, per diem, home trip allowance, and travel days, per the agreed rate card.
✓
Invoicing — consolidated monthly invoice to NES Canada itemizing contractor payments and Wide Brazil's fee.
