Section 01
Client Context & Recommended Structure
Understanding the situation and our proposed approach

Your client — a German company — is looking to engage a Brazilian national who will work remotely from Brazil as a Crane Manager, providing remote technical advisory and support. The worker requires formal employment, with a target net salary of €1,500 per month.

Because the German company has no legal entity in Brazil, it cannot directly employ a worker under Brazilian law. Any arrangement that bypasses formal employment would expose both parties to significant legal and financial risk.

Our recommendation: Wide Brazil acts as the legal Employer of Record in Brazil. The worker is employed under a standard CLT contract (Consolidação das Leis do Trabalho — Brazil's labour framework), with all statutory rights, benefits and tax obligations properly handled. Your client retains full operational control of the worker's day-to-day activities.

This structure is the only compliant path for a foreign company to engage an employed worker in Brazil without registering a local entity. It is widely used by international companies operating in Brazil, and is recognised by Brazilian tax and labour authorities.

Section 03
Employee Benefits
Mandatory entitlements and optional enhancements under Brazilian law

All employees hired under a CLT contract in Brazil are entitled to a defined set of statutory benefits. These are mandatory and cannot be waived. Wide Brazil manages and administers all of the below on your behalf — costs are passed through at actual cost, with no markup.

Meal Allowance (VR / VA)
Mandatory
R$ 24.80 per working day (approx. 22 days/month = R$ 545.60/month). Provided via a digital benefits card. Required under most collective agreements.
13th Salary (Gratificação Natalina)
Mandatory
An additional month's salary paid annually — first installment by 30 November, second by 20 December. Provisioned monthly across the year.
Paid Annual Leave
Mandatory
30 calendar days per year. Paid at 100% of salary plus a constitutional bonus of one-third (1/3) of the monthly salary, as required by Brazilian law.
FGTS (Severance Fund)
Mandatory
Employer deposits 8% of gross salary monthly into the employee's individual FGTS account. Accessible to the employee upon termination without cause, retirement, or specific life events.
Life Insurance
Mandatory
Group life insurance at approximately R$ 270/month. Required under most collective agreements in Brazil as a standard employer obligation.
Private Health Insurance
Optional
Not included in this simulation. Can be added upon request. Typical cost: R$ 600–1,200/month depending on age and coverage level. Wide Brazil can provide quotes.

Note on benefit costs: All mandatory benefits are pass-through items — Wide Brazil applies no markup. They are shown as line items in your monthly invoice so you have full visibility of what the employee receives.
