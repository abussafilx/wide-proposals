Section 01
Context & Recommended Structure
Understanding the situation and the right approach for Brazil

Nordic Brazilian Consulting operates at the intersection of Scandinavian and Brazilian business, providing consulting and advisory services from São Paulo. As the firm works with international clients and seeks to formalise or expand its employment footprint in Brazil, having a reliable, compliant employment infrastructure is essential — both for operational credibility and risk management.

Brazil's labour framework is governed by the CLT (Consolidação das Leis do Trabalho), one of the most structured and employee-protective labour codes in Latin America. Employment contracts, social contributions, mandatory benefits and termination procedures are all tightly regulated. For companies without a local entity — or those that have one but prefer not to manage the full HR and payroll burden internally — an Employer of Record is the standard, efficient solution.

Section 03
Employee Benefits in Brazil
Mandatory entitlements under CLT, plus market-standard optional benefits

All employees hired under the CLT are entitled to a standard set of statutory benefits. These are non-negotiable and apply from the first day of employment. All benefits are administered directly and included transparently in the monthly employer cost.

Meal / Food Allowance (VR/VA)
Mandatory
Provided via prepaid card (Alelo, Sodexo or equivalent). The standard rate is R$ 24.80 per working day — R$ 545.60/month based on 22 working days. Required by most collective bargaining agreements.
13th Salary (Décimo Terceiro)
Mandatory
An additional monthly salary paid in two instalments: 50% by 30 November, 50% by 20 December. 1/12 of gross salary is provisioned monthly to cover this obligation without year-end surprises.
Annual Leave (Férias)
Mandatory
30 calendar days of paid leave per year, plus a constitutional bonus of 1/3 of monthly salary at the time of vacation. The bonus is provisioned monthly — the base salary is already covered by regular payroll.
FGTS — Severance Fund
Mandatory
8% of gross salary deposited monthly into the employee's personal FGTS account. Funds are held by Caixa Econômica Federal and accessible upon dismissal without just cause, housing purchase, or retirement.
Life Insurance
Mandatory
Required under most collective agreements. Group life insurance coverage is provided at approximately R$ 270/month. Covers accidental death and permanent disability at a minimum.
Health Insurance (Plano de Saúde)
Optional
While not legally mandatory under CLT, private health insurance is a market-standard benefit and strongly expected by professional employees. A group health plan can be included — cost varies by age group and coverage tier. Available upon request.
Transportation Voucher (VT)
Mandatory
Employees who commute using public transport are entitled to a transportation voucher. The employer covers the amount exceeding 6% of the employee's base salary. Applicable to employees who require commuting support.
Dental Insurance
Optional
Increasingly standard in professional environments. Can be bundled with the health plan or provided separately. Coverage can be arranged through major Brazilian dental networks upon request.
Note on Benefits Costs
  • Mandatory benefits are pass-through in the monthly invoice — no markup applied.
  • Optional benefits (health, dental) are quoted separately and added to the invoice at cost.
  • The payroll simulation on the following page reflects mandatory benefits only.

Next Steps
How to move forward
1
Confirm Scope & Commercial Terms
Nordic Brazilian Consulting reviews this proposal and confirms the gross salary, any additional benefits required, and the number of employees to be onboarded. A formal service agreement is then issued for review.
2
Sign the EOR Service Agreement
A client services agreement is sent for review and signature. This document governs the relationship, invoicing terms, and data handling obligations. Typically completed within 2–3 business days.
3
Employee Onboarding
The onboarding team collects the employee's personal and professional documents, prepares the CLT employment contract, and registers the employee with the relevant Brazilian authorities (eSocial, FGTS, CTPS). The admissional health exam is scheduled and coordinated.
4
First Payroll Run
The first payroll cycle is processed on the agreed salary date. The client receives a payroll report and invoice in advance. All statutory deductions and benefit payments are handled automatically from this point forward.
5
Ongoing Operations
Monthly payroll, benefit administration, government filings, and HR support run on a fixed cycle. A dedicated contact is available for any operational or compliance questions throughout the engagement.
