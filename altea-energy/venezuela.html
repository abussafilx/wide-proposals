Section 02
Context & Scope of Services
EOR structure for Venezuela — Oil & Gas expatriate

Venezuela's labor framework under LOTTT is strongly protective of workers and includes mandatory provisions — utilidades, bono vacacional, and prestaciones sociales — that compound the annual employer cost significantly beyond the base salary. For Oil & Gas expatriates, the statutory structure is further layered by currency controls, the ISLR non-resident tax regime, and sector-specific contribution requirements.

✓

Payroll & Compliance

Section 03
Key Assumptions & Risks
Critical factors before final pricing
⚠ Preliminary — Employee Profile Required

This simulation is based on a generic expatriate profile. Final costs depend on the employee's nationality, role classification (particularly for sector-specific contributions), and confirmed tax residency. Wide Brazil will issue a revised simulation once these details are available.

⚠ OFAC Sanctions

US sanctions on Venezuela remain in force as of June 2026. General Licenses 46–55 have expanded Oil & Gas activity for US-incorporated entities, but restrictions persist broadly. Wide Brazil is not a US entity. If the client has US ownership or nexus, a compliance review is required before contract execution.

Currency & FX Risk

Venezuela's inflation is projected at ~629% for 2026 (IMF). Salaries referenced in EUR or USD are disbursed in VES at the BCV official rate on the payment date. The USD simulation figures will fluctuate monthly. Provisioning should be reviewed at least quarterly.

Provisioning Logic

The "Monthly provision" column shows the cash to set aside each month to cover all annual obligations — including year-end utilidades, vacation entitlements, and prestaciones. It is not the monthly payroll disbursement amount.
