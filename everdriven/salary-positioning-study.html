Market Positioning
CLT gross salary against the Brazilian tech market
Bottom line

The hypothesis holds. In gross CLT terms, EverDriven sits within — and often near the top of — the market on most roles. The exception is Tech Lead, now at the lower end of a higher market. Either way, the difficulty filling Senior DevOps is not the amount paid; it is the contracting model. Candidates compare take-home pay, and in that cut PJ and USD contractors put more in hand.

There is no gross-pricing problem on the individual-contributor tech roles — most sit in the upper range, and Mid-Level DevOps is above it. Tech Lead is the one to revisit, now at the market floor. The bottleneck is specific to Senior DevOps and stems from comparing take-home against alternative models — analysed in Section 02.

Section 03
Read & Recommendation
How to use this in the next round of offers
The central tension

What makes the candidate happy (PJ / USD take-home) is exactly what creates misclassification exposure in a full-time, subordinated, exclusive role — which is what a DevOps integrated into the team is. The wedge that CLT "costs" the candidate is, in practice, the price of compliance and protections (NDA, non-compete, IP) that the EOR delivers. It is not waste; it is the product.

Why DevOps is the worst case

It is the most internationally liquid profile. EverDriven, being a foreign company itself, recruits from the same pool that receives USD contractor offers. In other words: we compete in the hardest segment with the structure that shows the lowest apparent take-home. Frontend and mid-level roles do not feel the same pull, because they are less contested by dollar-denominated contracts.

Recommendation

Keep the CLT/EOR structure and close the gap through the total-value narrative first — present the ~R$ 33,000/month equivalent of the full package (gross + FGTS + 13th + vacation +1/3 + benefits + stability + enforceable protections), not the isolated take-home, which candidates routinely undervalue. Reserve a targeted gross-up as a one-off exception for Senior DevOps only (to ~R$ 31–35k) if the role keeps stalling — decided case by case, never as policy, and not extended to the other roles, which already sit at or above market. Route through COR/USD only where the role genuinely qualifies as independent; for a DevOps embedded in the team, it does not, and EOR/CLT remains the correct, defensible path.
