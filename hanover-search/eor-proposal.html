Section 01
Client Context & Recommended Structure
Understanding the situation and the right employment framework for Brazil

The Situation

Hanover Search is supporting a client with the hiring of IT professionals — including Project Manager roles — to be based in Brazil, with compensation at or above the equivalent of USD 60,000 per year. As placements are confirmed, each professional will need to be legally employed in Brazil without your client having to open and maintain a local entity.

Recommended Structure — Employer of Record

Wide Brazil recommends an Employer of Record (EOR) structure for these hires. Under this model, Wide Brazil becomes the formal legal employer of each professional in Brazil, issuing a compliant CLT employment contract, running payroll, and administering all statutory obligations — while the professional works exclusively under your client's day-to-day direction.

Why This Structure Is Appropriate

Compliance & Risk
  • Full CLT compliance managed by Wide Brazil
  • Employer liability sits with Wide Brazil, not your client
  • Statutory filings, benefits, and terminations handled correctly from day one
Speed & Simplicity
  • No need to incorporate a Brazilian entity
  • Onboarding can begin as soon as candidate data is confirmed
  • Scales cleanly — from a single hire to the full IT team
Note

This proposal outlines Wide Brazil's standard EOR structure, scope, and fee model for hiring in Brazil. A payroll cost simulation for one employee at an annual salary of USD 60,000, without optional benefits, is attached separately and should be read alongside this document.

Section 03
Employee Benefits in Brazil
What every employee receives — mandatory by law, plus optional enhancements

Brazilian labor law establishes a set of mandatory benefits for all CLT employees. These are pass-through costs — Wide Brazil administers them and invoices your client at exact cost, with no markup.

Meal Allowance (VR/VA)
Mandatory
Daily food/meal voucher, typically R$ 24.80 per working day. Paid via a benefit card; non-taxable for the employee.
13th Salary
Mandatory
One additional monthly salary paid annually, split across two installments (November and December).
Annual Paid Leave
Mandatory
30 calendar days per year, plus a constitutional 1/3 bonus on top of the vacation salary.
FGTS
Mandatory
Employer contribution of 8% of gross salary, deposited monthly into an employee severance fund account.
Life Insurance
Optional
Group life insurance, commonly added as a market-competitive enhancement for professional roles.
Private Health Insurance
Optional
Not statutory, but standard practice for IT and professional-level hires. Can be quoted on request.
This Proposal

The attached payroll simulation was prepared without optional benefits, reflecting only the mandatory items above. Optional benefits such as health insurance or life insurance can be added and re-quoted at any time.

Section 04
Pricing
Transparent fee structure — what your client actually pays Wide Brazil

Wide Brazil's EOR fee is straightforward and applies per employee. It has two components: a fixed monthly retainer, and a GST/overhead percentage applied to the total employer payroll cost. Statutory costs and benefits are always pass-through, invoiced at exact cost with no markup.

How This Applies

The fixed retainer and 8.5% GST apply per employee, regardless of headcount. As the number of professionals placed in Brazil grows — from a single Project Manager to the full team — each employee is invoiced under the same fee structure, with no setup fee. A full cost simulation for one employee at USD 60,000 per year is attached separately.

Volume Pricing

The fixed retainer reduces with scale. Above 5 concurrent EOR employees, the monthly retainer drops from USD 249 to USD 149 per employee, applied automatically once the threshold is reached.

Headcount Fixed Retainer (per employee/month) GST

6+ employees $ 149 8.5%

What's Included

  • No setup fee
  • Statutory employer costs (FGTS, INSS, RAT, Sistema S) are pass-through — no markup
  • Mandatory benefits are pass-through — itemized separately, no markup
  • GST covers local taxes and statutory charges applicable in Brazil

Assumptions & Next Steps
Conditions, exclusions, and the path forward

Assumptions & Exclusions

Assumptions
  • Employees are already resident in Brazil, or immigration support is scoped separately if relocation is required
  • Pricing is based on the standard EOR fee structure — USD 249 fixed retainer + 8.5% GST per employee, reducing to USD 149 above 5 concurrent employees
  • The attached simulation assumes an annual salary of USD 60,000 without optional benefits
Exclusions
  • Recruitment and candidate sourcing are not included in this proposal
  • Visa or immigration support is not included and would be scoped separately
  • Optional benefits (health insurance, life insurance) are not reflected in the attached simulation
