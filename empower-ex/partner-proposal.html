Section 01
The Partnership Context
How Wide Brazil and Empower-EX work together to deliver Brazil operations for Empower-EX's EOR client.

This proposal is addressed directly to Empower-EX as a commercial partner. The end client is an EOR company looking to establish a compliant legal entity in Brazil and operate it at scale — employing staff under CLT, running payroll, and potentially relocating executives through the Brazilian immigration system.

The structure is a partnership. Empower-EX manages the client relationship and the global EOR context. Wide Brazil delivers the Brazilian infrastructure — visible as a named partner — covering entity incorporation, legal representation, accounting, payroll BPO, and immigration support. Neither party is invisible in this arrangement: the client knows they are working with both Empower-EX and Wide Brazil.

Commercial model
BPO at scale
Pricing structured to grow with the end client's Brazilian headcount. No setup fee for incorporation is included in the partnership terms.

Wide Brazil is itself an active EOR operator in Brazil. We process CLT payroll, manage statutory compliance, and handle government filings for international companies every month. We are not consultants explaining how EOR works — we run the same model the end client is trying to build. Empower-EX can present this partnership with confidence that local execution will match the standard their own client expects.

Section 02
Scope of Services
What Wide Brazil delivers under the partnership — across every stage of the engagement, from incorporation through ongoing operations.
Entity Incorporation
✓
Company incorporation (Ltda. or S.A.) — full registration package including CNPJ, NIRE, state registration (Junta Comercial), and municipal registration (CCM). Entity type is confirmed at kick-off based on the end client's shareholding structure and business model.
✓
Articles of association — drafting of the contrato social or estatuto social in Portuguese, prepared in compliance with Brazilian corporate law and tailored to the end client's operational requirements.
✓
Tax regime election — guidance and registration under the appropriate federal tax regime (Simples Nacional, Lucro Presumido, or Lucro Real) based on the entity's projected revenues and operational profile.
✓
Legal representative appointment — a Brazilian-resident individual acting as the entity's formal representante legal, satisfying Receita Federal requirements for foreign-owned entities. This is a statutory role that enables the entity to operate under Brazilian law.
Ongoing Accounting & Compliance
✓
Monthly bookkeeping and financial statements — full accounting records maintained in accordance with Brazilian GAAP (CPC standards), including P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow statements.
✓
Federal, state, and municipal tax filings — DCTF, EFD-Contribuições, SPED Fiscal, SPED Contábil, and all ancillary obligations filed on time. No gaps, no penalties.
✓
Annual corporate income tax (IRPJ/CSLL) — preparation and submission of the ECF (annual tax return) and supporting documentation in compliance with Receita Federal requirements.

HR & Payroll BPO
✓
CLT employment contracts — drafting and ongoing maintenance of compliant employment agreements in Portuguese, covering all mandatory CLT clauses. Wide Brazil applies the same rigour here that Empower-EX would expect from their own EOR operations.
✓
Monthly payroll processing — full calculation of gross salary, INSS, FGTS, IRRF, and all statutory deductions. Processed with the accuracy and auditability an EOR business demands. Payslips distributed to employees monthly.
✓
eSocial & SEFIP filings — submission of all payroll-related government reports including eSocial events (S-1200, S-1210, S-2200, S-2299), DARF payments, and FGTS GFIP. Filed on schedule, every cycle.
✓
13th salary, vacation, and annual provisions — calculation and management of all statutory provisions including 13th salary (paid in November and December), vacation entitlements, and constitutional vacation bonus (1/3 of gross). Fully itemised in monthly reporting.
✓
Benefits administration — management of mandatory benefits (meal allowance, life insurance) and any optional benefits the end client elects to provide. All benefits are passed through at cost with full itemisation.
✓
Employee onboarding & offboarding — structured onboarding process covering documentation, registration with government systems, and benefits activation. Offboarding includes calculation of all termination entitlements and compliance with CLT dismissal procedures.
Immigration Support
✓
Work visa and residency applications — management of VITEM V (work permit) and CRNM (permanent residency) applications for foreign executives and senior employees employed by the entity. Each case is managed from document preparation through issuance.
✓
Investor visa support — assistance with CATIR/Resolução 13 investment visa applications where applicable. Complexity and timeline vary by case; pricing confirmed after eligibility review.
✓
Digital nomad visa (VITEM XIV) — processing for remote workers and digital professionals seeking legal residency in Brazil. Requirements and eligibility confirmed at intake.

Section 04
Next Steps
How to move this partnership forward — from alignment to entity live and operations running.
1
Partner Alignment Call — Empower-EX & Wide Brazil
Empower-EX confirms scope, fee structure, and any adjustments before the end client is formally brought in. Wide Brazil is available to join a call with the end client directly if that is useful — the partnership is visible, and so are we.
2
End Client Kick-off
A structured call — with Empower-EX and the end client — to confirm entity structure (Ltda. vs. S.A.), tax regime, legal representative requirements, and any immediate immigration needs. Time is not spent explaining how EOR works; both partners already know.
3
Services Agreement & Partner Onboarding
Execution of the services agreement between Wide Brazil and the contracting party (Empower-EX and/or the end client, depending on the commercial structure agreed). Wide Brazil provides a complete document checklist and incorporation timeline immediately upon signature.
4
Entity Incorporation
Wide Brazil initiates the incorporation process. Typical timeline is 15–45 business days depending on the Brazilian state selected and the completeness of shareholder documentation provided. Empower-EX receives milestone updates throughout.
5
Activation of Recurring Services
Once the entity is registered and the CNPJ is active, all recurring services go live: accounting and legal representation immediately, HR & Payroll BPO from the first employee onboarded. Immigration processing runs in parallel as soon as applicant documentation is available.
Key Assumptions & Exclusions
  • This proposal covers Wide Brazil's service fees only. Statutory employer contributions (INSS, FGTS, RAT, Sistema S), employee gross salaries, and mandatory benefits are pass-through costs billed at cost with full itemisation.
  • Government registration fees, notarial services, apostille, and certified translations required for incorporation are not included in the USD 2,000 setup fee and will be invoiced at cost with supporting documentation.
  • Immigration service fees are quoted per applicant at case initiation. Government filing fees and third-party costs are invoiced separately at cost.
  • Entity structure (Ltda. or S.A.) and tax regime to be confirmed at kick-off based on the end client's shareholding structure and projected revenue profile.
  • Wide Brazil's BPO scope does not cover tax litigation, regulatory disputes, or labor court proceedings. These are out of scope unless separately agreed in writing.
  • This proposal is valid for 30 days from date of issue.
