Section 01
Purpose & Context
Why a standard fee framework, and how it works alongside bespoke quoting

Following the recent request for information and the ongoing development of our partnership, Black Mountain asked Wide Brazil to define standard commercial terms across the core services delivered through the partnership: Employer of Record (EOR), Payroll, Recruitment and Work Visa support.

This document sets out that standard framework. Its purpose is to give both teams a clear, predictable baseline for quoting standard opportunities quickly, while preserving the flexibility to move to a bespoke quotation whenever the scope, volume, jurisdiction or risk profile of an opportunity falls outside what is described here.

For each service line, this framework defines four things: the scope covered by the standard fee, the assumptions and exclusions that apply, any volume thresholds relevant to pricing, and the circumstances that would trigger a bespoke quotation instead of the standard fee.

How to use this framework — For any incoming opportunity, check the relevant service section against the assumptions listed. If the opportunity fits within those assumptions, the standard fee applies directly and a quote can be issued without further validation from Wide Brazil. If it does not — for example, a country outside current coverage, an executive-level hire, or a volume outside the defined range — route it to Wide Brazil for a bespoke quotation.

Services Covered

  • Employer of Record (EOR) — compliant employment without a local entity
  • Payroll — payroll processing and benefits administration for clients with their own local entity
  • Recruitment — retained search and candidate placement
  • Work Visa & Immigration Support — work permits and residence authorization for foreign nationals

Country Coverage

Wide Brazil currently operates across Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay and Chile. The standard fees in this framework are designed as a consistent baseline across this footprint. Statutory costs — social charges, taxes and mandatory benefits — vary by country and are always passed through at cost; Wide Brazil's own fee structure does not change by country unless stated otherwise.

These are baseline figures — Every fee in this framework is a standard starting point for quoting. Final pricing can be revised case by case depending on employee volume, role complexity, and the overall scope of the project.

Section 03
Employer of Record (EOR)
Compliant employment in Brazil and LATAM without a local entity
Standard Fee
USD 299/month + 8.5% GST/Overhead
GST/Overhead is applied to the total employer payroll cost (gross salary + statutory social charges + mandatory benefits + annual provisions). It is Wide Brazil's own service charge — not an external tax. The fixed monthly component can be reduced to as low as USD 149/employee/month depending on employee volume.
No local entity required — Under the EOR model, Wide Brazil acts as the legal employer on the client's behalf. This means Black Mountain's clients can hire in Brazil, Argentina, Uruguay or Chile without incorporating a local entity, opening a payroll structure, or taking on direct employment liability — while retaining full day-to-day direction of the employee's work.

✓
Monthly payroll processing and statutory tax filings
✓

✓
Ongoing labor law compliance and risk management
✓
Onboarding and offboarding administration
✓
Dedicated HR point of contact
Assumptions & Exclusions
—
Standard, non-executive roles under a locally compliant contract type
—
One country of employment per engagement; no cross-border payroll splitting
—
Excludes legal entity formation, accounting and legal representation services
—
Excludes optional benefits beyond the statutory minimum unless quoted separately

Volume & Service Levels

The standard fee starts at USD 299/employee/month and can be reduced to as low as USD 149/employee/month for higher-volume engagements. Exact volume tiers are confirmed with Wide Brazil at the time of quoting. Onboarding is typically completed within 5–10 business days of a signed contract and complete documentation. Monthly payroll runs follow the statutory calendar of each country.

Bespoke Quote Triggers

  • Client requires local entity formation, accounting or legal representation
  • Executive or C-level hires requiring non-standard contract structuring
  • Regulated industries requiring additional compliance layers (e.g. financial services, healthcare)
  • Non-standard benefits packages beyond the statutory minimum

Once documentation is complete, filing with immigration authorities typically follows within 90 calendar days. Total time to approval varies by visa category and authority workload, and can extend if additional documentation is requested.
