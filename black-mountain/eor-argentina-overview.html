Section 01
Hiring in Argentina — What It Costs
Project details are still to be confirmed. This overview uses an illustrative gross salary of USD 5,000/month to walk through the statutory charges and benefits that apply to any employee hired in Argentina.

Wide LATAM employs the professional through its own operational Argentine entity, acting as the registered employer of record. This removes the need for Black Mountain to set up a local entity while keeping the employment relationship fully compliant with Argentine labor law. Below is a breakdown of the mandatory employer contributions that apply on top of gross salary, and the statutory benefits the employee is entitled to.

Mandatory Employer Contributions (on gross salary)
Jubilación (Pension)10.77%
Employer contribution to the national pension system, calculated on gross salary less a small fixed statutory deduction (Ley 27.541).
INSSJP (PAMI)1.59%
Funds the national retirees' healthcare institute (PAMI). Mandatory on every registered employment relationship.
Fondo Nacional de Empleo0.94%
National Employment Fund, financing unemployment insurance and job-training programs.
Asignaciones Familiares4.70%
Family allowances fund — finances child, marriage and other family-related statutory benefits paid via ANSES.
Obra Social (Health Plan)6.00%
Mandatory contribution to the employee's health insurance plan (Obra Social), calculated on full gross salary.
ART (Work Accident Insurance)2.89%
Workplace accident insurance premium — variable rate depending on role risk classification; 2.89% is the standard office-role rate.

Plus two small fixed monthly amounts: Fondo Fiduciario (ART fixed component) and mandatory life insurance (Seguro de Vida Obligatorio) — together under ARS 2,200/month, immaterial at this salary level.

Section 02
Employee Benefits & Deductions
What the employee receives, and what is withheld from gross salary to arrive at net pay.
Mandatory Benefits
Aguinaldo (13th Salary)
Mandatory
Paid in two installments (June and December), each equal to 50% of the highest monthly salary in the semester. Shown as a monthly provision in the Section 03 simulation.
Vacaciones (Paid Leave)
Mandatory
14 to 35 calendar days per year depending on tenure, paid at full salary. Shown as a monthly provision in the Section 03 simulation (14 days/year assumed for <5 years' tenure).
Obra Social (Health Plan)
Mandatory
Access to the national health insurance system, funded jointly by employer (6%) and employee (3%) contributions.
ART Coverage
Mandatory
Full workplace accident insurance, fully employer-funded, covering medical treatment and disability compensation.
Private Health Upgrade
Optional
Many employers top up the mandatory Obra Social with a private prepaid medicine plan (e.g. OSDE, Swiss Medical) — typically USD 80–150/month, quoted separately if desired.
Meal / Remote Work Allowance
Optional
Not a statutory requirement in Argentina, but a common market practice for competitive packages — can be structured as a non-remunerative allowance if desired.
Employee-Side Deductions (from gross to net)
Aporte Jubilación11%
Employee's own pension contribution, withheld from gross salary.
Aporte INSSJP + Obra Social3% + 3%
Employee's contribution to PAMI and to their own health plan.
All three employee-side deductions are calculated on gross salary up to a statutory cap (ARS 4,414,652.38/month as of this proposal). Above the cap, the deduction amount stays flat even as gross salary increases.
