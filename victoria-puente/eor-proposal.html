Section 03
Employee Benefits in Brazil
What every employee receives — mandatory by law, and optional enhancements

Brazilian labor law establishes a comprehensive set of mandatory benefits for all CLT employees. These are pass-through costs — Wide Brazil administers them and invoices the client at exact cost, with no markup.

Meal Allowance (VR / VA)
Mandatory
Daily food voucher, typically R$ 24.80 per working day (22 days/month = R$ 545.60). Paid via Sodexo, Alelo or equivalent card. Governed by collective bargaining agreements per sector.
13th Salary
Mandatory
One additional monthly salary paid annually, split into two installments (November and December). Provisioned monthly at 1/12 of gross salary to ensure predictable cash flow in monthly invoices.
Paid Annual Leave
Mandatory
30 calendar days of paid vacation per year, plus a constitutional bonus of 1/3 of monthly salary on top. Vacation bonus provision is accrued monthly at 1/3 ÷ 12 of gross salary.
Life Insurance
Mandatory
Group life insurance covering death and permanent disability. Standard policy approximately R$ 270/month. Required by law or by sector collective bargaining agreements in most industries.
FGTS — Severance Fund
Mandatory
Employer deposits 8% of gross salary monthly into a government-managed fund held in the employee's name. Accessible upon dismissal without cause, retirement or certain life events.
Transportation Voucher (VT)
Mandatory
Employer subsidises public transportation for the employee's commute. The employee contributes up to 6% of salary toward the cost; the employer covers the remainder. Included in benefits admin.
Private Health Insurance
Optional
Not mandatory by law, but frequently offered to attract white collar talent. Cost varies significantly by provider, age profile and coverage level. Wide Brazil can source and administer if required.
Dental Insurance
Optional
Common in professional and managerial roles. Can be bundled with health insurance. Available as an add-on and administered by Wide Brazil as a pass-through at cost.

Note for Chinese clients: Brazil's statutory benefit package is comprehensive by international standards. The effective employer cost for a CLT employee is typically 55–65% above the gross salary, once social charges and provisions are accounted for. Next Steps & Assumptions
How to move forward and what we need from you
1
Share the manufacturing position list
For the Minas Gerais client, send us the detailed list of roles, headcount, and target salaries. This allows Wide Brazil to produce a specific blue collar recruitment quote and confirm EOR cost simulations per role.
2
Define the pilot employee or role
As discussed, we recommend starting with a single pilot — either a white collar hire for the commercial client in São Paulo, or the first EOR activation. No minimum contract size is required. The pilot validates the workflow between our teams before scaling.
3
Agree on the partnership framework
Wide Brazil will prepare a simple partner agreement confirming pricing tiers, confidentiality, and operating procedures. This protects both parties and ensures consistency as new clients are introduced.
4
Begin onboarding or recruitment kick-off
Once a mandate is confirmed, Wide Brazil can initiate EOR onboarding (typically 5–7 business days for documentation) or begin the recruitment process for white collar roles (shortlist in 3–4 weeks).
5
In-person meeting in São Paulo (when ready)
When Mariana travels to Brazil, Wide Brazil's São Paulo team will be available to meet in our offices — or for a lunch — to strengthen the partnership and discuss any active mandates in detail.

Assumptions & Exclusions: This proposal covers white collar EOR and recruitment in Brazil. Blue collar recruitment fees require a separate quote based on the position list. Optional employee benefits (health insurance, dental) are not included in the cost simulation and are costed separately. Immigration and visa support for Chinese nationals is a separate scope item. All pricing is in USD; BRL amounts convert at the exchange rate prevailing on the invoice date. Wide Brazil fees do not include local taxes payable by the partner or client in their own jurisdiction.
