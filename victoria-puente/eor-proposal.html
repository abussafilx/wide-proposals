03
Employee Benefits

What every employee receives in Brazil.

Mandatory by law, and optional enhancements — all pass-through, no markup.

Brazilian labor law establishes a comprehensive set of mandatory benefits for all CLT employees. These are pass-through costs — Wide Brazil administers them and invoices at exact cost, with no markup.

Meal Allowance (VR / VA)

Mandatory

Daily food voucher, typically R$ 24.80 per working day (22 days/month = R$ 545.60). Paid via Sodexo, Alelo or equivalent card. Governed by collective bargaining agreements per sector.

13th Salary

Mandatory

One additional monthly salary paid annually in two installments (November and December). Provisioned monthly at 1/12 of gross salary for predictable cash flow.

Paid Annual Leave

Mandatory

30 calendar days of paid vacation per year, plus a constitutional bonus of 1/3 of monthly salary on top. The bonus is accrued monthly at 1/3 ÷ 12 of gross salary.

Life Insurance

Mandatory

Group life insurance covering death and permanent disability. Standard policy approximately R$ 270/month. Required by law or sector collective bargaining agreements in most industries.

FGTS — Severance Fund

Mandatory

Employer deposits 8% of gross salary monthly into a government-managed fund in the employee's name. Accessible upon dismissal without cause, retirement or certain life events.

Transportation Voucher (VT)

Mandatory

Employer subsidises public transportation for the commute. The employee contributes up to 6% of salary; the employer covers the remainder. Included in benefits administration.

Private Health Insurance

Optional

Not mandatory by law, but frequently offered to attract white collar talent. Cost varies by provider, age profile and coverage level. Wide Brazil can source and administer if required.

Dental Insurance

Optional

Common in professional and managerial roles. Can be bundled with health insurance. Available as an add-on and administered by Wide Brazil as a pass-through at cost.

Note for Chinese clients: Brazil's statutory benefit package is comprehensive by international standards. The effective employer cost for a CLT employee is typically 55–65% above the gross salary, once social charges and provisions are accounted for. Next Steps

From proposal to first hire.

How to move forward and what we need from you.

1

Share the manufacturing position list

For the Minas Gerais client, send us the detailed list of roles, headcount and target salaries. This allows Wide Brazil to produce a specific blue collar recruitment quote and confirm EOR cost simulations per role.

2

Define the pilot employee or role

We recommend starting with a single pilot — either a white collar hire for the commercial client in São Paulo, or the first EOR activation. No minimum contract size is required. The pilot validates the workflow between our teams before scaling.

3

Agree on the partnership framework

Wide Brazil will prepare a simple partner agreement confirming pricing tiers, confidentiality and operating procedures. This protects both parties and ensures consistency as new clients are introduced.

4

Begin onboarding or recruitment kick-off

Once a mandate is confirmed, Wide Brazil can initiate EOR onboarding (typically 5–7 business days for documentation) or begin the recruitment process for white collar roles (shortlist in 3–4 weeks).

5

In-person meeting in São Paulo (when ready)

When Mariana travels to Brazil, Wide Brazil's São Paulo team will be available to meet in our offices — or for a lunch — to strengthen the partnership and discuss any active mandates in detail.
