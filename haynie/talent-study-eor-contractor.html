The Brief
Context & Objective

You are building out a growing paid-media function and want to add one person who combines media buying and creative strategy in a single role. Ad spend has scaled from roughly US$ 30K/month to US$ 70–80K/month over the past 5–6 months, with paid-social budgets expected to reach US$ 200K–300K/month as the business grows.

The priority is not the raw budget number on a résumé — it is a proven track record of actively scaling paid-media programs, hands-on daily platform expertise (Meta first, then TikTok, then LinkedIn), and the judgment to turn day-to-day performance data into the next creative brief. The person reports to your Senior Media Buyer and works closely with your creative and video teams.

The additional requirement that shapes everything The role demands fluent English and close collaboration with a US-based lead. That single filter changes both the candidate pool and the salary benchmark — and it is exactly where Latin America becomes a strong alternative to the Sri Lanka option currently on the table.
02
Rationale
Why Brazil & Chile

Talent depth

Brazil has built a large pool of performance and growth-marketing talent — one of the world's biggest digital-ad markets. Chile adds a smaller but strong, well-educated Santiago-based pool.

Time-zone & culture

Both countries sit on or near US business hours and share a Western, direct working culture — the initiative and push-back you told us was missing elsewhere.

English-fluent segment

Fluent English is scarcer here, so it carries a premium — but the professionals who have it are used to working with international teams remotely.

This proposal lays out three ways to bring this person on board, so you can weigh cost, candidate calibre, and compliance side by side — and take a clear, defensible recommendation to your team.

Model 1 · EOR (Cost-Optimized)

A compliant full employee, calibrated to land close to the Sri Lanka price point — with an honest note on the candidate trade-off.

Model 2 · EOR (Senior Bilingual)

A compliant full employee at the market rate for the exact profile you described — fluent, seasoned, a proven scaler.

Model 3 · Contractor via Uruguay

An independent-contractor structure with lower tax load, engineered for belonging and performance while managing classification risk.

The English premium is real Fluent English is scarce in both markets — only a small share of professionals reach an advanced level. For a role where English is a hard requirement, that scarcity pushes the effective salary toward the top of the local range plus a premium, and it shrinks the candidate pool. Market research places the premium anywhere from ~30% at operational levels to well over 50% at coordination and management levels.

Reference point
The Sri Lanka Benchmark

The Sri Lanka option you are evaluating quotes the professional at US$ 3,700 – 4,500/month. That figure is a useful anchor. The models on the following pages are built around it — showing where Latin America matches it, where it costs more, and what you get for the difference: a professional who is fluent, time-zone aligned, and culturally close to your US team, rather than technically strong but distant on all three.

What drives cost up

  • Employer social charges (Brazil ≈ 36.8% of salary)
  • English + seniority premium and scarcity
  • Mandatory benefits & annual provisions

What keeps Chile competitive

  • Employer charges only ≈ 9.75% of salary
  • Lower local base in USD terms
  • Relevant if you are relocating to Chile

Our View
Recommendation & Next Steps
Our recommendation For the profile you described — fluent, seasoned, a proven scaler working daily with your US lead — Model 2 in Chile (≈ US$ 4,839/month) is the strongest fit: right calibre, full compliance, close to the Sri Lanka price, and aligned with your move to Chile. Keep Model 3 as the option if maximizing the professional's take-home and reducing tax load matters more than the compliance simplicity of an EOR. Brazil remains the choice when you want access to the deepest talent pool and are comfortable with the higher cost.
